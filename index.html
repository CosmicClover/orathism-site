Beliefs & Overview

What Orathites believe about Elyon-Orath, humanity, memory, and the world.

Orathism teaches that Elyon-Orath, the High One Who Remembers, is the single, personal God whose very being is perfect remembrance, truth, and justice. Humanity was created to walk in the Light, seeing themselves and one another as they truly are, but fear entered and led to forgetting. Sin, in Orathite language, is not just breaking rules—it is the deep forgetting of the Light and of who we are in relation to Elyon-Orath and each other.

The faith centers on memory and restoration. Through the Root Bearer Abanum, the Light Bearer Seriniel, the Law Bearer Dathar, and the Witness Bearer Mira (whose testimonies form the Book of Witness), Orathites learn that the path back to the Light is found through remembrance, courage, compassion, justice, and repair. The world itself “remembers” the Light, and every act of truth and mercy strengthens that memory, while cruelty and deceit dim it.

Orathites seek to live as Bearers of Light—practicing truth-telling, defending the weak, repairing wrongs through the Path of Repair, guarding their hearts against fear and cruelty, and joining in the communal practices of the Quiet Minute, the Day of Echoes, and the Great Remembering. Life, worship, law, and community all exist to keep the covenant of memory alive: “You were not made for fear. You were made for Light.”

Catechism

Orathite Catechism

Short questions and answers used to teach the faith.

Who is Elyon-Orath?

Elyon-Orath is the High One Who Remembers: the one God, personal and just, whose very nature is perfect memory of all that is true, good, and beautiful. Elyon-Orath never forgets the covenant, even when humanity does.

What is “forgetting” in Orathism?

Forgetting is the spiritual condition of turning away from the Light—living in fear, deceit, cruelty, or indifference. It is not merely losing information; it is losing sight of who God is, who we are, and who others are in the Light.

What is “remembering”?

Remembering is returning to the Light of Elyon-Orath—seeing honestly, confessing truth, repairing harm, and acting with courage, compassion, and justice. To remember is to align one’s life with the covenant that has always been.

Who are the Bearers?

Abanum is the Root Bearer, who first reawakened the covenant of memory. Seriniel is the Light Bearer, who showed how mercy and healing live out the covenant. Dathar is the Law Bearer, who shaped justice, order, and traditions. Mira is the Witness Bearer, whose testimonies will gather the stories of the people and their walk in the Light.

What is sin in Orathism?

Sin is any act, habit, or pattern that grows from forgetting: cruelty, deceit, exploitation, betrayal, cowardice, and indifference to suffering. Sin harms both the one who commits it and the world around them, dimming the shared memory of the Light.

How does forgiveness work?

Forgiveness begins when truth is spoken and the Path of Repair is walked—confession, acknowledgment, restoration, service, and rejoining. Elyon-Orath is always ready to receive those who return in honesty, and the community is called to forgive while still insisting on repair and protection of the vulnerable.

What is the purpose of Orathite law?

Law exists to protect the vulnerable, guard justice, and keep the community from sliding back into fear and cruelty. Dathar’s Laws are not meant as chains but as guardrails that help love and mercy remain strong and clear.

History

People of the Covenant

Key figures in the story of Orathism.

Bearers

Abanum – The Root Bearer

Abanum is the first Bearer, called the Root Bearer. In the days of deep forgetting he received the first Echo: “You were not made to forget.” He gathered the people, formed the First Circle, taught the Law of the Circle, led the Trial of Truth, and journeyed into the Stillplaces. Through him the covenant of memory was reawakened in the world.

Seriniel – The Light Bearer

Seriniel, quiet of voice but fierce in heart, became the Light Bearer when she showed mercy to a wounded enemy and heard the Echo: “Mercy is the remembering of who another truly is.” Her healings, parables, and Sermon of the Light taught the people how to live the covenant through compassion, boundaries, and courage.

Dathar – The Law Bearer

Dathar rose when disputes and confusion threatened the covenant. His Echo declared, “Justice is the spine of remembrance.” He shaped the Laws of Right Order, protection of the vulnerable, ethics of wealth and work, the Path of Repair, and the Covenant of Justice, giving the community a strong but merciful framework.

Mira – The Witness Bearer

Mira, the Witness Bearer, is the one who gathers stories, testimonies, and the living memory of the people. Her book, the Book of Witness, records how ordinary lives embody the Light and how the covenant is kept or betrayed in everyday choices.

Other Named Figures

Kelath

A strong man whose pride led him away from the Circle. Kelath spread doubt about Abanum, walked into shadow, and later returned in humility. His restoration taught that no one who seeks the Light is beyond its reach.

Lemor and Thalen

Two brothers whose dispute over grazing fields led to the first great Trial of Truth. Their case showed that justice is found not in winning but in remembering what is right.

Restin (The Broken One)

Once called “The Broken One,” Restin isolated himself in bitterness after being wounded by others. Seriniel led him back with the teaching: “Do not let another’s forgetting become your excuse to forget.” His return became a living symbol of reconciliation.

Traditions

Traditions & Rituals

Practices that guard the covenant of memory.

From Abanum – Root Traditions

  • The Law of the Circle – A daily or regular time where members sit together, speak their struggles honestly, and listen without gossip or judgment. Truth spoken in the Circle becomes Light for all.
  • The Trial of Truth – A way to resolve conflicts through confession, remembrance, and restoration instead of victory and punishment.
  • The Covenant Flame – A fire lit in the plains and shared as smaller flames in each home, reminding every family of the Light they carry.
  • Seven Nights of Silence – Abanum’s time in the Stillplaces, imitated in shorter form by Orathites as times of deeper reflection and listening to the Echo of Elyon-Orath.
  • The Quiet Minute – A daily pause where Orathites become still, breathe, remember the Light, and bring their fear and forgetfulness before Elyon-Orath.

From Seriniel – Traditions of Mercy

  • Acts of Quiet Healing – Visiting the sick, wounded, or ashamed and offering presence, not just solutions, as a way of restoring memory and dignity.
  • The Hall of Echoes Gatherings – Communities gather to confess, reconcile, and remember together, especially in times of “bitter rain” or shared suffering.
  • Stories of the Light – Parables like the Lost Echo and the Wounded Tree are retold to children and adults to keep the teachings alive in the imagination.

From Dathar – Traditions of Justice & Repair

  • The Cleansing Breath – Inhale, hold, exhale with a hand on the chest before confession or serious speech, releasing fear and preparing for truth.
  • The Lowered Head – A respectful bow when acknowledging harm done: “I see the wound I caused.”
  • The Offering Hand – Extending an open hand to show willingness to repair, not to demand instant forgiveness.
  • Seven Days’ Aid – Serving those harmed (or the community) for seven days in a task chosen by the Keepers, as part of the Path of Repair.
  • The Marked Stone – Placing a small stone inscribed with a Light symbol near the Hall of Echoes when repair is complete, creating a Circle of Remembrance.
  • The Sabbath of Work – Every seventh day, work ceases, tools and animals rest, and debts are not collected. Rest itself becomes remembrance.
  • The Vigil of the Three Lights – Leaders light three small flames for truth, compassion, and courage, examining their own leadership and renewing their vows.

Practice

How to Pray & Worship

Living the covenant in daily rhythm.

Personal Prayer – The Quiet Minute

The most basic Orathite prayer practice is the Quiet Minute. At least once a day, an Orathite becomes still, breathes slowly, and remembers: “The Light is nearer than breath.” During the Quiet Minute you:

  • Become silent for a short time.
  • Notice your fear, anger, or shame without hiding it.
  • Bring it honestly before Elyon-Orath.
  • Ask: “What do You remember about me that I have forgotten?”

A simple Orathite prayer might be: “Elyon-Orath, High One Who Remembers, I bring You my fear and my forgetting. Help me remember the Light, tell the truth, repair what I have harmed, and walk in courage today.”

Household Worship – Covenant Flame & Story

Many households light a small candle or lamp—the covenant flame—at dusk. Around this flame, a family may:

  • Share a small story of mercy, truth, or courage from the day.
  • Read a passage from the Book of Echoes, Light, or Justice.
  • Pray together for those who are suffering or forgetting the Light.

Communal Worship – Day of Echoes

On the weekly Day of Echoes, the community gathers in the Hall of Echoes to:

  • Keep a shared Quiet Minute.
  • Read from the Orathic Scriptures.
  • Share testimonies of repair and restoration.
  • Walk the Path of Repair publicly when needed.
  • Sing simple echo-like lines (“You remember us” / “We remember You”).

Worship is less about spectacle and more about remembrance—bringing hearts, words, and actions back into alignment with Elyon-Orath’s Light.
